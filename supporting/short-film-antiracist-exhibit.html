Presented as supporting work because it adds community storytelling and public-facing media production to the technical portfolio.

Overview

This project connects documentary interview work with anti-racist visual communication, using media production as a way to support public dialogue and community-centered storytelling in Tacoma.

Context

The work is connected to an anti-racist art exhibit and community interview context at the University of Washington Tacoma, including an interview subject with civic leadership significance as Tacoma's deputy mayor.

Distinction

The project carries additional weight through its exhibit and award context, showing that the work was not only produced for class but also positioned for public presentation and recognition.

Process

The project required planning an interview structure, shaping a public-facing visual narrative, editing motion media, and presenting the final piece as part of a broader anti-racist communication initiative.
